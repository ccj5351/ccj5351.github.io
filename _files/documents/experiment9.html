1. Introduction:

  • This experiment aims at analysing and compressing the indices which represent the mapping from any patch in the original images to its correspondence in the epitome.
  • The indices are represented by the 2-D (row, column) coordinate, of the top left corner of the corresponding patches in the epitome.
  • The indices are visualized for analysis, down-sampling and reconstruction.
  • This experiment learned 74 images of the Edwin building at Stevens, with the same size of 1024 by 768 or 768 by 1024 for each image.

3. Result Analysis

  • 1. Why is the visualization of the indices roughly like the original image? The reason is that patches with similar texture in the original image will be mapped into the same region in the epitome, thus those patches will have the nearly same row or column indices.
  • 2. Down-sampling can halve the index file size. And the reconstruction via interpolating the down-sampled indices is very similar to the original indices.
